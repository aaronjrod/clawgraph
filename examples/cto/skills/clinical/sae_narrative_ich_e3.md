# ICH E3: Patient Narratives for Serious Adverse Events (SAE)

## Section 1: Narrative Mandate
Provide a chronological, standalone medical account for every Serious Adverse Event (SAE), death, or significant clinical event of interest.

## Section 2: Standard Narrative Structure

### 2.1 Demographics & History
- **Subject ID**: [Unique Identifier]
- **Age/Sex**: [XX / Male|Female]
- **Relevant Medical History**: Chronic conditions, prior surgeries, risk factors.
- **Concomitant Medications**: All therapy active at the time of the event.

### 2.2 Study Drug Exposure
- **Investigational Product**: Drug Name.
- **Dosing Regimen**: Amount, frequency, route.
- **Timing**: Study Day and Cycle relative to event onset.

### 2.3 Event Chronology
- **Onset Date**: [DD-MMM-YYYY]
- **Clinical Course**: Step-by-step progression of symptoms.
- **Laboratory Data**: Key markers with baseline and peak/trough values (include normal ranges).
- **Interventions**: Emergency treatments, hospitalisation details.

### 2.4 Action & Causality
- **Action Taken**: None, Interrupted, Discontinued, Dose Reduced.
- **Causality Assessment**: Investigator's opinion vs. Sponsor's assessment.
- **Outcome**: Resolved, Resolved with Sequelae, Ongoing, Fatal.

## Section 3: Prose Standards
- Use objective, clinical terminology.
- Maintain strict chronological order.
- Do not include Protected Health Information (PHI) like names or initials.
